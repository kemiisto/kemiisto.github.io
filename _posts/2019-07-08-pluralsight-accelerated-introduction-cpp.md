 - **Title**: [Accelerated Introduction to C++][course]
 - **Author**: Dmitri Nesteruk
 - **Publisher**: Pluralsight
 - **Year**: 2014
 - **Duration**: 5h 17m

 - **Rating**: ★★★★★
 - **Recommended**: as an introduction to modern C++ for experienced programmers familiar with "conventional" (i.e. class-based) OOP.
 - **Not recommended**: for beginners in programming.

The course is intended to be a *short* intro to the *modern* C++ for developers already familiar with another OOP language such as C# or Java and it is fairly good at this particular task. The course is well-structured and covers just enough areas of C++ to start from, istructor's explanations are concise and clear and his articulation is excellent.

Just remember that C++ is an *insanely big* language with tons of *unique non-trivial features*, so that any single video course that is just few hours long would only *skim the surface* of the wide and deap "C++ ocean". One necessarily need to continue his or her studies of C++ after this course since it is highly *unlikely* that even an experienced Java or C# programmer will be able to write or even fully comprehend any *real-world* C++ program after listening to this only (or any other similar) course.

[course]: https://www.pluralsight.com/courses/accelerated-introduction-cpp
